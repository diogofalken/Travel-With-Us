New York

City in New York

New York City comprises 5 districts located on the Hudson River and Atlantic Ocean. In the center of the city is Manhattan, a district with a high population density that is among the world's major commercial, financial and cultural centers. Between its emblematic points, it emphasizes skyscrapers, like Empire State Building, and the enormous Central Park. The Broadway Theater sits in the neon lights of Times Square. More

The Brooklyn Bridge connects Manhattan to the Brooklyn borough with several neighborhoods such as Williamsburg (an indie music, art and nightlife center) and Coney Island with its amusement parks. In the Queens district, is the Flushing Meadows Corona Park, with the Unisphere sculpture, built for the 1964 World's Fair, in the shape of a globe and the height of a 12-story building. The Bronx, New York's most upscale district, houses the New York Botanical Garden and the Bronx Zoo. The Staten Island Ferry connects Manhattan to the southernmost district of New York and offers a panoramic view of the city's silhouette and the Statue of Liberty.
