Madrid

Capital of Spain

Madrid, the capital of Spain, located in the center of the country, is a city of elegant avenues and large and well-kept parks, such as Buen Retiro. It is famous for the rich collections of European art, among which are the works of Goya, Velázquez and other Spanish masters at the Prado Museum. The center of old Habsburg-era Madrid is the Plaza Mayor, flanked by porticos, and nearby are the Baroque Royal Palace and the Royal Arsenal, which displays historic weapons. More

In the 1970s, Madrid became the epicenter of La Movida, an energetic movement of artistic expression. The Prado is part of the "Golden Triangle", along with the Reina Sofía Museum (home to Picasso's "Guernica") and the Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum. El Rastro, a Sunday flea market, is in the medieval neighborhood of La Latina, famous for its nightlife, theater and tapas bars. The central plaza Puerta del Sol is where the statue of the Bear and the Medronheiro, image of the coat of arms of the city, while the National Archaeological Museum exhibits a vast collection of historical artifacts. Real Madrid, the city's main soccer team, plays at the Santiago Bernabeu Stadium.
