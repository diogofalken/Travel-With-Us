London

Capital of England

London, the capital of England and the United Kingdom, is a 21st century city with a history dating back to the Roman era. Its center houses Parliament's imposing headquarters, the famous clock tower of Big Ben and Westminster Abbey, the coronation site of the British monarchs. Across the River Thames, the London Eye Giant has panoramic views. More

A 17th century design by the architect Sir Christopher Wren, St. Paul's Cathedral stands on the "City", where the London Stock Exchange conducts negotiations of global reach. Royal sites include Buckingham Palace, which houses the main residence and offices of the monarchy, and the Tower of London, a former prison guarding the crown jewels. The British Museum, National Gallery, Tate Britain and Tate Modern explore local and international history and art. The West End theaters feature classic and avant-garde pieces. Covent Garden, Soho and Spitalfields Market are very frequented areas with restaurants and shops. Trafalgar Square, Hyde Park and Hampstead Heath are other important public spaces.
