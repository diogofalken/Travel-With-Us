Tokyo

Capital of Japan

Tokyo, the bustling capital of Japan, combines ultramodern style with traditional, from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples. The opulent Shinto shrine Meiji is known for its towering gate and the surrounding forests. The Imperial Palace is located amidst public gardens. The city's many museums offer exhibits ranging from classical art (at the National Museum of Tokyo) to a reconstructed kabuki theater (at the Edo-Tokyo Museum). More

The ancient narrow streets of the Asakusa district have shops, kimono women and the 7th-century Sens≈ç-ji Buddhist temple. Roppongi has bustling nightclubs and karaoke bars, while Akihabara houses state-of-the-art electronics stores. Cozy Japanese-style pubs, called izakaya, are scattered throughout the city. The Tsukiji fish market, with a famous tuna auction, is close to the center. The Tokyo Skytree Tower offers panoramic views of the city from its public observation deck. Tokyo is famous for its vibrant gastronomic scene, and the Shibuya and Harajuku districts are at the core of a modern young fashion culture.
